The [Stasis Device](https://nomansskyrecipes.com/products) and [Fusion Igniter](https://nomansskyrecipes.com/products) both sell for **15.6 million credits** — the highest value of any craftable item in No Man's Sky. Same price, same grind, but the path to each is completely different.

If you're building a credit farm, the right choice depends entirely on what resources you have easiest access to. Here's a side-by-side breakdown.

Both share the Quantum Processor — which itself is a significant craft requiring Circuit Boards and Superconductors — and both need the same quantities of the three atmospheric gases.

## Which Is Actually Easier?

**The short answer: Fusion Igniter is slightly easier — but only if you have a Fungal Mould farm.**

Here's why.

### Stasis Device: More Crop Variety, Harder Setup

The Stasis Device needs **six different crop types** across six different planet biomes. Gamma Root (radioactive biomes) and Faecium (toxic biomes) are the annoying ones — 400 and 50 units respectively. If you don't have farms dedicated to those, you'll be planet-hopping to harvest them manually, which takes time.

It also needs **150 Ionised Cobalt** — triple the Fusion Igniter's requirement. That's not a dealbreaker, but it adds up across multiple crafts.

The mineral side is actually straightforward: Paraffinium, Phosphorus, and Dioxite are all common planetary resources found on lush, hot, and frozen planets. If you know where to look, you can mine 50 of each in a few minutes.

### Fusion Igniter: Fewer Crop Types, One Big Hurdle

The Fusion Igniter only needs **five crop types** and skips the Gamma Root and Faecium requirements entirely. That alone makes the farm setup simpler — one fewer biome to deal with.

The tradeoff is **600 Fungal Mould**. That's the single largest crop requirement of either item, and Fungal Mould only grows on toxic planets. If you have a Fungal Mould farm already running, this is a non-issue. If you don't, harvesting 600 units manually is a serious grind.

**Mordite** (25 units) sounds intimidating if you haven't farmed it before, but it's trivial in practice — just kill a handful of creatures on any planet and you're done.

**Pure Ferrite** (200 units) is also easier than it looks. Ferrite Dust refines 1:1 into Pure Ferrite and Ferrite Dust is everywhere. You'll accumulate more than enough just playing normally.

### The Verdict

| Scenario | Easier Choice |
|---|---|
| You have farms for all crop types | **Fusion Igniter** (one fewer crop type) |
| You don't have a Fungal Mould farm | **Stasis Device** |
| You're a new player starting fresh | **Stasis Device** (more forgiving mineral requirements) |
| You have a toxic planet base | **Fusion Igniter** (Fungal Mould is free) |
| You want to farm both | Build for Stasis Device first — Gamma Root and Faecium are the hardest farms to establish |

If you're building from scratch with no existing farm, the Stasis Device is slightly more forgiving. Paraffinium, Phosphorus, and Dioxite are common on standard planet types — you'll find them everywhere. The Gamma Root requirement is annoying, but 400 units from a farm isn't that bad once it's set up.

If you already have a multi-biome crop farm and just need to decide which to prioritise, go with the Fusion Igniter. Less crop diversity means less base complexity and fewer farm plots to maintain.

---

## The Case for Farming Both

Once your farm is fully operational, there's no reason to choose. The Quantum Processor is shared between both crafts — you need it either way — and the gases are identical. The only difference is which set of crops and minerals you collect per session.

Many players alternate between the two depending on which materials hit capacity first. Run Stasis Devices until you're out of Gamma Root, then switch to Fusion Igniters while the Gamma Root grows back. It keeps your resource stockpiles balanced and doubles your crafting throughput.

## Getting the Blueprints

All intermediate blueprints are available from the Synthesis Laboratory on the Space Anomaly for Nanites. The top-level blueprints (Stasis Device and Fusion Igniter) unlock through the advanced crafting milestone chain — you'll need to craft a certain number of advanced products to trigger them.

If you're missing any specific blueprint, the [crafting guide](https://nomansskyrecipes.com/crafting-guide) lists all sources.
