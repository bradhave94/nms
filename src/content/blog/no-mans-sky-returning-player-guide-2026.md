No Man's Sky launched in August 2016 as a controversial solo experience with big ambitions and a rough delivery. It's now April 2026, nearly ten years and 40+ free updates later. The game you remember and the game that exists today are not the same game in any meaningful way.

This is the complete history of every major update, what each one actually changed, and a practical guide for anyone coming back after time away.

## What each major update actually changed

### Foundation — November 2016

The first real content update after a rocky launch. [Foundation](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added base building to No Man's Sky for the first time, letting players construct structures on any planet using gathered resources. It also introduced two new game modes: Creative (unlimited resources, no survival pressure) and Survival (much harsher conditions), alongside purchasable freighters. The conversation around the game shifted after this. Players who had bounced off the launch experience came back to check it out.

### Pathfinder — March 2017

[Pathfinder](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added the Exocraft, planetary land vehicles. The Roamer, Nomad, and Colossus gave players a much faster way to cover ground on foot. It also introduced ship class ratings (C, B, A, S), online base sharing through a community upload system, and the first PS4 Pro optimisation pass.

### Atlas Rises — August 2017

[Atlas Rises](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) was the first update that seriously reworked the core game. It added a brand new story (the Atlas storyline), dramatically improved terrain generation, a new alien race in the Korvax lore, and portals, letting players teleport to specific planet coordinates shared by other players. The first form of multiplayer appeared here: you could see other players as floating orbs in the same system, though you couldn't directly interact.

### NEXT — July 24, 2018

[NEXT](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) is the update that saved No Man's Sky. Full multiplayer arrived. Players could now explore, build bases, and interact together properly. Third-person perspective became the default camera. The entire game received a major graphical overhaul. No Man's Sky launched on Xbox for the first time. Base building expanded massively. If you left the game before NEXT and haven't been back, almost everything you know about the game is wrong now.

### The Abyss — October 2018

[The Abyss](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) turned the oceans of No Man's Sky into something worth diving into. New underwater creatures, buildings, and resources made aquatic exploration a real activity. It came with its own short horror narrative, something quite different in tone from the rest of the game.

### Visions — November 2018

[Visions](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) introduced new biome types and much greater visual diversity in planets. The universe became stranger and more colourful. Crashed freighters appeared on planet surfaces for the first time, and a new discoveries catalogue tracked everything you'd found across your travels.

### Beyond — August 2019

[Beyond](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) was billed as No Man's Sky 2.0. VR support arrived, fully playable in virtual reality from start to finish. The Space Anomaly became a proper multiplayer hub where players could gather in large groups. The NPC economy improved significantly. Online sessions expanded to 32 players in the same space. The Quicksilver currency and its cosmetic reward shop appeared. If NEXT rebuilt the foundation, Beyond opened the doors.

### Synthesis — November 2019

[Synthesis](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) was mostly quality-of-life work that made the game feel more polished. Ship scrapping let you break down unwanted ships for parts and units. Technology transfer between ships became possible. The inventory UI improved. A lot of things that had felt awkward got smoothed out.

### Living Ship — February 2020

[Living Ship](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added a completely different ship class: organic spacecraft that grow and evolve rather than being upgraded with standard technology. Obtaining one required hatching a Void Egg through a long questline. The resulting ship has unique abilities and a distinct visual style unlike anything else in the game. They're still a popular choice for players who want something different.

### Exo Mech — April 2020

[Exo Mech](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added the Minotaur, a mech-style exocraft that could equip hazard protection, fight Sentinels, and harvest resources without the pilot needing to leave the cockpit. For players on extreme planets, it became invaluable. The terrain manipulator got significant upgrades at the same time.

### Crossplay — July 2020

[Crossplay](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) removed the barriers between platforms. PlayStation and Xbox players could finally explore together, along with PC. A small update on paper, but significant for anyone who wanted to play with friends on different hardware.

### Desolation — July 2020

[Desolation](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added derelict freighters: massive abandoned ships drifting in space, infested with hostile robots and environmental hazards. Boarding them for rare loot, cargo, and narrative logs became one of the best activities in the game. The tension of navigating a dark, broken freighter corridor is unlike anything else NMS offers.

### Origins — September 2020

[Origins](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) overhauled the universe. Planets became dramatically more diverse, with weirder terrain, more extreme weather, and a much greater range of biome types. New creatures appeared. Volcanoes, planetary rings, new atmospheric effects, and stranger world types made exploration feel genuinely surprising again. If you left before Origins, the planets you land on now will barely resemble what you remember.

### Next Generation — November 2020

[Next Generation](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) brought No Man's Sky to PS5 and Xbox Series X. 60fps gameplay, ray tracing, dramatically faster load times, and 4K resolution. Existing players on the new hardware got a free upgrade. The game looked and ran significantly better.

### Companions — February 2021

[Companions](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) expanded the creature system that had existed in a limited way since launch. Players could now adopt fauna as proper companions, name them, level them up, and breed them using the Egg Sequencer aboard the Space Anomaly. Pets could be taught to mine resources, produce materials over time, and act as mounts. This laid the foundation for the creature system that Xeno Arena built on in 2026.

### Expeditions — March 2021

[Expeditions](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added a new game mode: time-limited community events where all players start fresh on the same planet and complete shared milestones together for exclusive rewards. Expeditions have run regularly since and typically last four to six weeks. Missing one usually means missing its exclusive cosmetics, though Hello Games has run redux versions of popular ones. The Expedition Terminus on the Space Anomaly is where you start or rejoin them.

### Prisms — June 2021

[Prisms](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) was a pure visual upgrade. Reflective surfaces, volumetric lighting, better textures, creature fur, new sky effects, and improved warp effects made the game look dramatically better without changing any mechanics. It was one of those updates where screenshots from before and after looked like different games.

### Frontiers — September 2021

[Frontiers](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added Settlements, alien villages populated by NPCs that players could discover, claim, and manage. As settlement overseer, you make decisions for the population, resolve disputes, manage resources, and gradually build the settlement up. A fully upgraded settlement generates passive income and becomes a second base of operations. The build menu also got a major rework in this update.

### Sentinel — February 2022

[Sentinel](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) completely reworked how the game's law enforcement enemies work. New Sentinel types, new AI behaviour, new combat mechanics. The update also added Hardframe Sentinel mechs as enemies, and as companions after defeating them. A new storyline uncovered the origins of the Sentinels. If you remember Sentinels as simple drones that you ignored, they're a much more formidable and interesting system now.

### Outlaws — April 2022

[Outlaws](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added a criminal underbelly to the galaxy. Outlaw star systems have pirate-run space stations with their own economy, contraband items, and missions. Solar ships became a new ship class. Pirate raids on freighters became more organised and dangerous. The update added a full smuggling economy and new story content around the outlaw factions.

### Endurance — July 2022

[Endurance](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) completely reworked freighters. Freighter interiors became fully customisable with base-building parts. New frigate expedition mechanics, improved fleet management, and new freighter types made the capital ship side of the game worth engaging with seriously. Freighters in Endurance feel like proper flying bases.

### Waypoint — October 2022

[Waypoint](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) was the Nintendo Switch launch update, but its biggest change was a complete rework of the inventory system. The old grid-based, size-limited inventory was replaced with a unified slot system. Tech, cargo, and general slots became clearly separated. The game got adjustable difficulty settings for the first time, letting players tune survival mechanics, resource gathering, and combat to their preference. If you left before Waypoint and your old save still exists, your inventory will look completely different when you load it.

### Fractal — February 2023

[Fractal](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added PSVR2 support with a full visual overhaul for VR. It also introduced the Wonders catalogue, a persistent record of the most impressive or unusual things you've discovered across the universe, from the tallest creature to the largest planet.

### Interceptor — April 2023

[Interceptor](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added Corrupted Sentinel ships as a new buildable ship class, assembled from parts found on Dissonant (corrupted) planets. These planets have a distinctive glitch-red aesthetic and unique enemies. The Sentinel ships themselves are some of the most visually distinct spacecraft in the game.

### Echoes — August 2023

[Echoes](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) introduced the Autophage, a new robot race with their own culture, dialogue, and lore. Finding and helping them unlocked new story content and cosmetics. Pirate freighters became capturable for the first time. Board one, fight through it, and claim it as your own capital ship. New missions and improved multiplayer functionality came alongside.

### Orbital — March 2024

[Orbital](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) redesigned every space station in the game. New architecture, more NPC variety, new traders, and new mission types. More importantly, Orbital added full ship building from modular components, the same system that Corvettes would later use. You could now hunt for specific ship parts from salvage and assemble a custom vessel rather than finding a pre-built one.

### Worlds Part I — July 2024

[Worlds Part I](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) was the second major planet overhaul. New water technology made oceans look and behave more realistically. New cloud systems, new terrain generation, new fauna types including giant alien megafauna. The variety of planet types expanded significantly. Players who had explored extensively before this update found familiar systems suddenly populated with different terrain and new creatures.

### Aquarius — September 4, 2024

[Aquarius](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added fishing. Over 200 fish species spread across 8 biome types, a Fishing Rig Multi-Tool attachment (1 Nanite from Iteration Eos), an Exo-Skiff water platform, Automated Fish Traps, and a full range of new seafood recipes for the [Nutrient Processor](/cooking). The depth mechanic (shallow water for small fish, deep water for large and colossal) added more strategy than you'd expect. See our [full fishing guide](/blog/no-mans-sky-fishing-guide) for everything you need to know.

### The Cursed — October 2024

[The Cursed](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) was a Halloween expedition with its own unique mechanics around cursed planetary conditions and corrupted fauna. It ran for roughly three weeks and introduced some exclusive cosmetics that were later available in the Holiday 2024 expedition redux.

### Cross-Save — November 2024

[Cross-Save](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) is one of the most practically useful updates for returning players. No Man's Sky now supports cross-save and cross-play across every platform: PC (Steam, GOG), PlayStation 4 and 5, Xbox One and Series, Game Pass, Nintendo Switch, Steam Deck, and PSVR. You can start on PC and continue on console, or switch platforms entirely without losing progress.

### Worlds Part II — January 2025

[Worlds Part II](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) expanded the universe with entirely new planet types. Gas giants became visitable for the first time: massive swirling worlds with unique atmospheric conditions and resources. Water worlds (planets almost entirely covered in ocean) appeared alongside new underwater terrain. New star types brought new system aesthetics. A new story thread ran through the update alongside ancient ruins scattered across planets.

### Relics — March 2025

[Relics](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added fossil hunting. Using a scanning tool in Relic sites on planets, you can locate and excavate the skeletal remains of ancient alien creatures. Completing specimens rewards rare materials and lore. Settlement mechanics also improved with this update, with more building variety and management options.

### Beacon — June 2025

[Beacon](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) launched No Man's Sky on Nintendo Switch 2 with significant visual improvements. Settlement features got another round of improvements with more decision variety and new building options. New base building parts expanded the construction toolkit.

### Voyagers — August 27, 2025

[Voyagers](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added Corvettes, the biggest ship type in the game. Built module by module at the Corvette Workshop in any Space Station, Corvettes are massive ships with walkable interior sections. You can install refiners, storage, and decoration inside. Crew them with other players in multiplayer. Scan planets from the cockpit without landing. A lot of players now use them as flying bases. See our [full Corvette guide](/blog/no-mans-sky-corvette-guide) for how to build and upgrade one.

### Breach — October 2025

[Breach](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) expanded Corvette content with new module types and added corvette wreck sites in deep space: zero-gravity salvage missions where you can strip parts from abandoned Corvettes floating between stars.

### Remnant — February 11, 2026

[Remnant](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added the Gravitino Coil, a gravity manipulation attachment for the Multi-Tool that lets you magnetise, carry, and launch physical objects. Industrial Waste appeared on scrap planets, collected by Coil and processed at new Waste Processing Plants. The Colossus exocraft received a major rework with customisable cabins, chassis options, paint finishes, and new modules including a flatbed for hauling waste.

### Xeno Arena — April 8, 2026

[Xeno Arena](https://www.nomanssky.com/release-log/) added creature battles. Adopt wild fauna, assemble a team of three, and fight at Holo-Arena tables found at space stations, planetary outposts, and the Space Anomaly. The system has 8 elemental affinities, a full genetic upgrade system, an Arena League faction with ranked medals, and daily challenges from the new NPC Iteration: Oceanus. See our [full Xeno Arena guide](/blog/no-mans-sky-xeno-arena-guide) for everything you need to know.

## What you'll find when you return, by era

How much has changed depends entirely on when you left.

| You left around... | What you'll recognise | What will be completely new |
|---|---|---|
| Launch (2016) | The basic loop: mine, refine, craft, sell | Multiplayer, base building, pets, settlements, fishing, Corvettes, creature battles — basically everything |
| NEXT era (2018–2019) | Multiplayer, third person, basic base building | Space Anomaly as a hub, VR, Companions, Expeditions, overhauled planets, all post-2020 content |
| Beyond era (2019–2021) | Space Anomaly, companions, expeditions | Sentinel rework, Outlaws, new ship classes, Waypoint inventory system, Echoes races, all 2022+ content |
| Waypoint era (2022–2023) | New inventory system, difficulty settings, Frontiers settlements | Echoes Autophage race, ship building, Worlds planet overhaul, fishing, Corvettes, Xeno Arena |
| Worlds era (mid 2024–mid 2025) | Planet overhaul, fishing | Worlds Part II gas giants/water worlds, Relics fossils, Corvettes, Remnant Gravitino Coil, Xeno Arena |
| Voyagers era (Aug 2025 – Mar 2026) | Corvettes, Breach salvage | Remnant Gravitino Coil, Colossus rework, Xeno Arena creature battles |

## What to do when you first load back in

Regardless of when you left, do these things before anything else:

**1. Check your technology slots for broken upgrades.** Major updates sometimes invalidate old tech. Anything showing a warning icon is costing you stats for nothing. Dismantle broken tech and reinstall where needed.

**2. Visit the Space Anomaly.** It's the game's main hub and it gets new NPCs with most major updates. Talk to Iteration: Oceanus to pick up the Xeno Arena intro quest. Check the Quicksilver Synthesis Companion for any new cosmetics. Look at the Expedition Terminus to see if a current expedition is running.

**3. Check the Wonders Catalogue.** It tracks your catch records, discoveries, and accomplishments. If you have companions, check their status. They may need feeding.

**4. Look at your settlement.** If you claimed one before leaving, it's been making decisions without you. Check the management panel and clear any queued issues.

**5. Scan your current system.** The Analysis Visor gets new things to find with most major updates. Turn on Creature Survey Mode if you plan to start creature battles. It overlays battle traits before you adopt anything.

## What still works exactly the same

The core economy loops haven't changed. If you had a working setup before, it still works:

**Chlorine loop:** 1 Chlorine + 2 Oxygen refines into 6 Chlorine. Feed the output back as input and multiply your stack. Sell at any Galactic Trade Terminal for millions of units. Still intact, still the fastest units farm for most players. Browse the full [refiner recipes](/refining) for this and other loops.

**Nanite farming:** Refine Runaway Mould from Curious Deposits, or break down Tainted Metal. Both still work. See the [Nanite farming guide](/blog/best-nanite-farms-no-mans-sky) for the current most efficient methods.

**Freighter economy:** Frigate expeditions still run on timers and return resources and units. If you had a strong fleet before you left, they've been sitting idle waiting to be sent out again.

**Base building fundamentals:** The build menu expanded considerably since Frontiers, but everything you built before still stands. New parts are added with most updates.

## The three systems worth learning immediately

These are the biggest additions since 2022 that returning players will know nothing about.

### Fishing

Added in Aquarius (September 2024). Get the Fishing Rig blueprint from Iteration Eos at the Space Anomaly for 1 Nanite, craft it, and you can fish in any body of water. Over 200 [fish species](/fish) across 8 biomes, with [rare](/items?group=Rare+Fish) and [legendary fish](/items?group=Legendary+Fish) selling for up to 200,000 units each. The depth mechanic lets you control what size fish you catch. Full breakdown in the [fishing guide](/blog/no-mans-sky-fishing-guide).

### Corvettes

Added in Voyagers (August 2025). The Corvette Workshop inside any Space Station lets you build a massive modular ship from parts. You walk around inside it, decorate it, install refiners, and crew it with other players. It costs roughly 7.6 million units to buy all the basic parts from the Workshop, but salvaging from scrap planets cuts that down significantly. Full breakdown in the [Corvette guide](/blog/no-mans-sky-corvette-guide). Browse all [corvette parts](/corvette) on the site.

### Xeno Arena

Added April 8, 2026. Adopt wild creatures, build a team of three, and fight at Holo-Arena tables across the galaxy. Eight elemental affinities determine matchups. Fire beats Frost, Radioactive beats Fire, and so on. Rewards include Nanites, Retroviral Pellets for upgrading creature genetics, and exclusive companions from Iteration: Oceanus. Full breakdown in the [Xeno Arena guide](/blog/no-mans-sky-xeno-arena-guide).

## Should you start a new save?

It depends on when you left and how attached you are to your progress.

**Start fresh if:** you left before Waypoint (October 2022). The inventory system changed so significantly that your old save will feel like driving with the wrong controls until you adjust. Starting fresh also gives you access to the Expedition game mode from the beginning, and the introductory quests properly introduce systems like Corvettes and Xeno Arena that your old save will skip past.

**Keep your old save if:** you left after Waypoint. The inventory change won't affect you, your bases and ships will be intact, and your Nanites and units are still there. You'll just need to catch up on the new systems, which is what this guide is for.

Either way, Cross-Save (November 2024) means you can now play on any platform without losing progress. If you want to pick up on a different device, that's now possible.
