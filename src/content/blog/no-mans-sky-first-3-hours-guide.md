No Man's Sky drops you on a random planet with a broken ship and almost no explanation. It's intentional, but it can feel overwhelming. The good news is the first three hours are actually pretty focused — if you know what to prioritize.

Here's what to do, in order.

---

## Hour 1: Get off the planet

This is the tutorial, and it's the one part of the game you should actually follow.

### Fix your multi-tool first

Your scanner is broken. Fix it. You need it to locate resources on the planet surface. The game will tell you what materials to collect, and they're all right nearby.

Once the scanner is working, use it constantly. Hold the scan button and watch the HUD populate with icons for minerals, plants, and points of interest. Everything in yellow is worth collecting early.

### Repair the ship

Your Launch Thrusters and Pulse Drive are both damaged. You can't leave without them. The required materials vary by starting planet — Ferrite Dust, Di-hydrogen, and Carbon are common asks — but the repair prompts walk you through each step.

A few things worth knowing before you wander off. Don't go far from your ship until you've fixed the scanner — without it, finding your way back is annoying. The first storm usually hits while you're still scrambling for ship parts; caves and your cockpit both count as shelter. Life support drains constantly, and Carbon is what refills it. Grab more than you think you need.

Once the ship is repaired, take off. You're done with the starter planet.

---

## Hour 2: Learn the actual game

Leaving atmosphere triggers the Artemis Path — the main story questline. Follow it. This is the most important advice in this guide.

A lot of new players ignore the quest log once they hit space. They explore randomly, get lost, run out of fuel, and quit. The Artemis Path exists specifically to stop that from happening.

### What the Artemis Path teaches you

The quest line walks you through every major system in the game:

- Building a base
- Installing a Hyperdrive
- Using Teleporters
- Learning alien languages
- Visiting the Space Anomaly

None of it is long or tedious. It moves quickly and gives you clear goals when the open world feels directionless.

### The Space Anomaly is a big deal

You'll meet the Space Anomaly early in the Artemis Path. Board it. Explore it. Talk to Nada and Polo.

The Anomaly is the game's multiplayer hub, but it's also where you unlock blueprints using **Salvaged Data** — items you dig up from buried technology deposits on planet surfaces. The Construction Research Station inside the Anomaly lets you spend Salvaged Data on new base building parts, farming blueprints, and exocraft.

You can summon the Anomaly at any time from your quick menu while in space. Use it often.

### Scan everything

Every planet you visit, use your Analysis Visor on every creature, plant, and mineral you see. Each scan earns Units. It feels trivial at first. With S-class scanner upgrade modules installed later, a single rare creature scan can pay over 100,000 Units. The habit is worth building now.

---

## Hour 3: Set up a base and get your bearings

The Artemis Path will prompt you to place a Base Computer and start building. Do it.

Your first base doesn't need to be elaborate. Think field outpost, not dream home. You want a shelter — any enclosed structure keeps storms from chewing through your hazard protection. Drop a Save Point, because the game's auto-save isn't always where you'd want it. Build a Portable Refiner early; it converts raw materials into useful ones (Ferrite Dust into Pure Ferrite, Cobalt into Ionised Cobalt) and those conversions come up constantly. The Teleporter is the one that changes how freely you can explore — once it's linked to a space station, you can warp back to base from anywhere in the galaxy at no cost.

### Dig up Salvaged Data

Buried technology sites show up on your HUD as small blue icons once you're on a planet surface. Use your Terrain Manipulator to dig them up. Each one gives you Salvaged Data. Spend it at the Space Anomaly's Construction Research Station.

Do this on every planet you visit. It adds up fast, and the blueprints you unlock — especially for farming and storage — open up parts of the game that would otherwise stay locked for hours.

### Talk to aliens

Every space station has several alien NPCs. Talk to all of them. Each conversation teaches you a word in their language — Gek, Korvax, or Vy'keen — and builds your reputation with their faction. Better faction standing means better trade prices and access to more dialog options.

You won't understand much at first. That's fine. The understanding builds over time, and early conversations still count.

---

## What not to bother with yet

A few things that will grab your attention early but aren't worth the time right now.

Freighters: you'll get a free freighter rescue mission in the first few systems. Accept the ship, dismiss it, move on. Don't spend Units upgrading it yet. Freighters matter a lot later; right now it's just a free asset sitting in orbit.

S-class ship hunting is a trap at this stage. You'll see better-looking ships at space stations. Don't sell your starter ship to buy one you can't afford to upgrade. Your starter ship is fine for the first few hours.

When the Artemis Path gives you a choice between continuing it and starting the Atlas Path, pick Artemis. The Atlas Path is slow and folds back on itself. Artemis teaches more systems and moves faster.

---

## The three-hour checklist

- [ ] Scanner repaired
- [ ] Ship repaired and airborne
- [ ] Artemis Path started
- [ ] Space Anomaly visited
- [ ] Base Computer placed
- [ ] Portable Refiner built
- [ ] Teleporter built and linked
- [ ] Salvaged Data spent at Construction Research Station
- [ ] Scanner visor used on at least one full planet's worth of fauna

Three hours in, you should have your own base, access to the Space Anomaly, at least a few blueprints unlocked, and a reasonable grip on why everything works the way it does. After that, the game opens up into whatever you want it to be.
