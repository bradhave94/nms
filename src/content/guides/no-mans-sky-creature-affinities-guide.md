Creature affinities are the backbone of successful Xeno Arena battles in **No Man's Sky**. If you understand the matchup chart, you can counter dangerous enemy teams, choose better companions, and avoid bringing creatures into losing fights.

If you need the raw chart first, start with the dedicated [Creature Affinities](/creatures/affinities) page. This guide explains how to actually use those matchups in battle.

## How creature affinities work

Every battle-ready creature in the arena system is assigned an affinity. That affinity determines:

- which opponents it counters
- which opponents it struggles against
- which move naming variants and icons may appear
- how you should build a team around it

Matchups matter most when two creatures with opposing strengths face each other directly. Winning more often is usually less about raw stats and more about entering battles with the right counter affinity.

## Full affinity matchup chart

These are the core strength and weakness relationships:

- **Fire** → strong against Cold, weak against Radioactive
- **Cold / Frost** → strong against Desert, weak against Fire
- **Radioactive** → strong against Fire, weak against Toxic
- **Toxic** → strong against Tropical, weak against Mechanical
- **Mechanical** → strong against Toxic, weak against Anomalous
- **Anomalous / Weird** → strong against Mechanical, weak against Desert
- **Desert / Barren** → strong against Radioactive, weak against Frost
- **Tropical / Lush** → strong against Anomalous, weak against Toxic

Use the [creature affinities page](/creatures/affinities) whenever you want a quick reference during team building.

## How to choose the right affinity

When deciding which companion to bring into the arena:

1. Identify the enemy team's likely main affinity.
2. Choose a creature whose affinity is strong into that matchup.
3. Review its move set on the species detail page.
4. Keep a second creature ready in case the battle rotates to an unfavorable matchup.

The best teams are rarely built around one creature. They are built around **coverage**.

## Best practical tips for arena battles

### 1. Build around counters, not favorites

Your favorite pet might not be your best battler. A weaker creature with the right affinity advantage can outperform a stronger-looking creature in the wrong matchup.

### 2. Learn your bad matchups

Knowing what your creature loses to is just as important as knowing what it beats. If your team is vulnerable to Toxic or Radioactive counters, you should plan swaps before the fight starts.

### 3. Use species pages to inspect battle readiness

Not every creature is battle-ready. Use the [Creature Species](/creatures/species) directory and individual species pages to confirm:

- battle readiness
- affinity
- rarity
- move area
- move set source

### 4. Keep a balanced roster

A strong roster usually includes:

- one safe opener
- one dedicated counter option
- one backup creature that covers your primary weakness

That gives you more flexibility than stacking similar affinity choices.

If you want better Xeno Arena results in **No Man's Sky**, start by learning the affinity wheel. The fastest way to improve is to stop taking neutral or losing matchups and begin building teams that deliberately counter the enemy lineup.
