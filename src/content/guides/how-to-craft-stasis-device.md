The **Stasis Device** is one of the signature late-game crafting goals in **No Man's Sky**. Players search for it because it combines high-value production, endgame progression, and one of the clearest examples of the game's layered manufacturing chains.

If your goal is to stop guessing and build a repeatable production plan, this guide gives you the simplest workflow.

## Why the Stasis Device matters

Players usually craft Stasis Devices for three reasons:

1. to create a **high-value product** for selling
2. to build a clear **endgame crafting objective**
3. to understand how advanced **manufacturing chains** fit together

Unlike simple products, a Stasis Device depends on multiple layers of intermediate components. That means the smartest strategy is not memorizing every ingredient. The smartest strategy is creating a reliable process.

## Best workflow for crafting a Stasis Device

Use this order:

1. Open the **[Crafting Guide](/crafting-guide)** to understand direct product relationships.
2. Open the **[Stasis Device item page](/products/STASISDEV)** for the item-specific breakdown.
3. Use the **[Crafting Calculator](/calculator)** to scale your run from one device to multiple devices.
4. Gather or refine the lowest-tier materials first, then move upward through your intermediate products.

That is the easiest way to avoid discovering one missing ingredient at the end of the chain.

## Break the chain into smaller jobs

The biggest mistake players make is trying to assemble everything at once. Instead, split a Stasis Device run into three layers.

### 1. Raw material gathering

Secure the base resources used across multiple branches first. These are usually the biggest time sink, so locking them in early makes the rest of the process smoother.

Helpful supporting pages:

- **[Raw Materials](/raw)** for source items
- **[Refining](/refining)** for conversion chains
- **[All Items](/items)** if you want to filter by type or search by name

### 2. Intermediate products

Once raw materials are secure, craft the components that appear repeatedly across advanced recipes. This is where the **[Products](/products)** section becomes especially useful.

### 3. Final assembly

After your intermediates are complete, use the Stasis Device item page and the calculator to build the final product with fewer surprises.

## How to scale from one Stasis Device to many

If your goal is profit, one craft is only the beginning. Use the **[Crafting Calculator](/calculator)** to:

- increase your output target
- view scaled ingredient totals
- identify which raw materials become the bottleneck

This makes it much easier to decide whether you should spend your time mining, refining, farming, or buying missing materials.
