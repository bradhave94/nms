The **Fusion Ignitor** is one of the headline late-game products in **No Man's Sky**. If your goal is to turn complex manufacturing into a reliable unit-making routine, this is one of the best products to build toward.

Like the Stasis Device, the Fusion Ignitor is not difficult because of one rare ingredient. It is difficult because the chain has multiple stages. The best strategy is to turn the recipe into a workflow instead of trying to memorise every branch.

## Why the Fusion Ignitor matters

Players usually chase Fusion Ignitors for three reasons:

1. to craft a **high-value product** for sale
2. to understand the game's **late-game manufacturing tree**
3. to build a repeatable process for profitable crafting sessions

If you approach it one ingredient at a time, the chain feels overwhelming. If you split it into planning layers, it becomes much easier to execute.

## Best workflow for Fusion Ignitor crafting

Use this order:

1. open the **[Fusion Ignitor product page](/products/ULTRAPROD1)**
2. review the wider **[Crafting Guide](/crafting-guide)** for prerequisite relationships
3. scale your target quantity in the **[Crafting Calculator](/calculator)**
4. gather base resources first, then assemble intermediate products

This workflow prevents the most common late-game crafting problem: reaching the final assembly step with one missing support item.

## Break the Fusion Ignitor chain into smaller stages

### 1. Gather base resources first

Before crafting any advanced products, make sure you can reliably source the raw materials used across several branches. The easiest supporting pages are:

- **[Raw Materials](/raw)**
- **[Refining](/refining)**
- **[Items](/items)**

This is the stage where a lot of wasted time disappears. Once your stockpile is stable, the rest of the chain becomes much more predictable.

### 2. Build intermediate products in batches

Most profitable late-game products are easier when you craft prerequisite components in batches rather than one at a time. That applies especially to:

- product subcomponents
- refined support materials
- repeatedly used crafting parts

The **[Products](/products)** section is the best place to move between those layers quickly.

### 3. Use the calculator before you commit

If you are crafting more than one Fusion Ignitor, the **[Crafting Calculator](/calculator)** is the simplest way to spot bottlenecks before you start. It helps you:

- scale total ingredient requirements
- identify which branch is slowing you down
- decide whether to mine, refine, or buy missing components

That matters because the real time cost of a Fusion Ignitor run is rarely the final craft. It is the hidden prep work underneath it.

## Fusion Ignitor vs Stasis Device

If you are deciding between late-game product goals, compare:

- **[Fusion Ignitor](/products/ULTRAPROD1)**
- **[Stasis Device](/products/ULTRAPROD2)**

Both represent advanced production chains, but the best choice depends on the materials and farming loops you already have in place. In practice, many players use both as part of a broader high-value crafting routine.

The best way to craft a Fusion Ignitor in **No Man's Sky** is to stop thinking of it as one recipe and start thinking of it as a system. Plan the batch, gather the shared materials first, build intermediate products in order, and use the calculator to keep the whole process efficient.
